Toxicity Rating Study

Rating Social Media Posts

ETH Zurich — Academic Research

What you will do

You will read 38 short social media posts written by members of the public about a recently released film. For each post, you will rate how toxic it is using a sliding scale from −50 to +50.

What we mean by toxic

A post is toxic if it contains language that a reasonable person would find attacking, devaluing, or harmful — or that disrupts civil, constructive discourse. Toxicity can be directed at the film, at the people who made it, or at other viewers. It does not matter whether you agree or disagree with the opinion expressed — only how the language itself comes across.

How to rate

Move the slider to any point between −50 and +50:

−50 Not at all toxic: respectful and civil, even if strongly opinionated
+50 Extremely toxic: severely abusive, hateful, or threatening

You will see this definition again at the top of each page. Please read each post carefully before rating. There is no time pressure.

Please complete the study in one sitting.

Do not refresh or close your browser during the task as your progress cannot be recovered.

Content warning

Some of the posts in this study contain toxic, offensive, or otherwise disturbing language. If you would prefer not to be exposed to such content, please return this study now.

By continuing, you confirm that you are 18 years or older and agree to participate voluntarily. Your anonymized responses will be used for academic research only. Questions? Contact hauke.roggenkamp@mtec.ethz.ch.
